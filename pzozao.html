pizza is an iconic Italian dish originating from Naples, consisting of flattened bread dough topped with ingredients like tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and olive oil, baked quickly at high temperatures. It evolved from ancient flatbreads into a global staple, featuring diverse styles—notably Neapolitan and American (New York/Chicago)—with over 5 billion pizzas sold annually.

key facts about pizza:

Origins: The modern pizza originated in the 17th century in Naples, Italy, as an affordable, quick meal for working-class citizens . Margherita Pizza: Created in 1889 by Raffaele Esposito to honor Queen Margherita of Savoy, it features tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to represent the Italian flag's colors.

Common Styles: 1.Neapolitan: Thin crust, soft, and cooked rapidly at very high temperatures. 2.New York Style: Large, foldable slices with a crispy outer crust. 3.Chicago Deep Dish: Thick, casserole-like pizza with crust lining a deep pan.
